NBA The Hall Of Fame

Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame

The Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame is a museam located in Springfield, Massachusets. This museam is also an Hall of Fame for all of basketball, awarding those who have shown massive contributions to basketball. Although coaches, referees, and anyone else who has had a major effect on the game can be placed in the Hall of Fame, we will only be examining the player category. in order to be a player placed in the Basketball Hall of Fame, one must have been retired for three years and have had a winshare (a value representing contribution to wins) of at least 50.

Our Dataset

For our main dataset, we chose to use a dataset on Kaggle containing player personal data along with performances every season including the variables below:

Total Points: Total number of points scored over their career
Win Share: Each win is worth 3 points. Player are given a part of the 3 points depending on the amount they contributed to that game. Win Share is cumulative.
Offensive Win Share: Win Share considering only offensive plays.
Defensive Win Share: Win Share considering only defensive plays.
Win Share/ 48 mins: Average Win Share for 48 minutes played
Player Effeciency Rating: A score that predicts player Effeciency using other statisitics
Basketball Box Score-Based Metric: A rating dependent on number of points scored over the average per 100 plays
Offensive Basketball Box Score-Based Metric: The same rating as above but only dependent on offensive plays
RPG: Rebounds per game
SPG: Steals per game
APG: Assists per game
BPG: Blocks per game

Along with these variables were other variables including number of championships, free point percentage,and three point percentage. However,we had decided that due to the incredibly weak coorelation between those variables and Hall of Fame, we would choose to stay away from considering them. The dataset contained data from 1950 to 2017 but we choose to only examine data beginning from 1977 due to extremly limited data starting from 1950 and the use use of exactly four decades. Although this data set is not perfect, lacking crucial data about major players who have shown up in the past 7 years, it is still contains a plethora of data.

The Goal

Our goal was to simply use the variables we could to do two major tasks. First we wanted to predict who would likely, post retirement, be entered into the Hall of Fame. Secondly, we wanted to find underrated players who have shown excellence in the games where they have been played but who have simply failed to have played in the number of games that would allow for them to have things such as high winshare.
